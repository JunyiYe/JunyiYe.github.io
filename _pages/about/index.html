Junyi Ye is a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the New Jersey Institute of Technology (NJIT), conducting research under the mentorship of Distinguished Professor Guiling (Grace) Wang. He holds a master’s degree in Computer Science from NJIT and a master’s degree in Optics from Shanghai University, where he studied under the guidance of Professor Ye Dai.

Junyi’s research interests lie at the intersection of large language models, graph neural networks, time series analysis, and multimodal reasoning. His work focuses on developing innovative AI frameworks for solving real-world challenges, including privacy-preserving information retrival, financial modeling, and explainable decision-making AI systems. He is particularly interested in exploring the generative and interpretive capabilities of AI in applications spanning finance, healthcare, and decision making.
